(WHO THIS GENRE IS FOR) If you enjoy learning about new things or gaining a deeper knowledge for things you thought you knew, this is the right genre for you. From the human universe to accounts on people's real lives, this genre explores it all.

Below we have our top rated Non-fiction books

THE BOOK IN FIFTH PLACE is Sapiens by Yuval Noah Harari. This book covers the whole of human history, how we first came to walk the earth to where we are now. This thought-provocing book allows us to challenge our perceptions and discover what makes us human and our future.

THE BOOK IN FOURTH PLACE is The Immortal Life Of Henrietta Lacks by Rebecca Skloot. This true story is about a poor African-American tobacco farmer and how her cells that were taken without her ever knowing, became one of the most important tools in medicine. Her cells were vital in developing the polio vaccine, viruses, IVF and so much that we know of cancer just to list a few. Yet she was never acknowledged and her family has never profited from this multimillion-dollar industry.

THE BOOK IN THIRD PLACE is The Diary Of A Young Girl by Anne Frank by Anne Frank. This is a book detailing the day to day life of thirteen year old Jewish girl who fled to Amsterdam during the Nazi occupation. She, along with her family, lived in an annexe for two years before being discovered. Her diary tells her story and was discovered in the same attic she spent those years.

THE BOOK IN SECOND PLACE is A Brief History Of Time by Stephen Hawking. A book considered a landmark in scientific writing that explores what we know about the origins of the universe and how it works. Since writing the original copy, a new introduction was added as well as recent research.

THE BOOK IN FIRST PLACE is A Long Walk To Freedom by Nelson Mandela. This book is about the life of Nelson Mandela, one of the greatest political leader of our time and Nobel Peace Prize winner. It follows his journey from his life as a young boy to his arrest during apartheid in his own country of South Africa.

Our top five Non-fiction books
  • #5 Sapiens: a Brief History of Humankind by Yuval Noah Harari
  • #4 The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks by Rebecca Skloot
  • #3 The Diary Of a Young Girl by Anne Frank
  • #2 A Brief History of Time by Stephen Hawking
  • #1 Long Walk to Freedom by Nelson Mandela
